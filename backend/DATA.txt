3. Student Services & Tools

NORSU provides several modern tools and offices to support student life:

    NORSU Regi-Quick: A mobile application and kiosk system designed to speed up transactions with the Registrar, such as viewing grades or requesting documents.

    CARE Center: The "Counseling, Assessment, Resources, and Empowerment" Center provides mental health support and guidance counseling.

    Medical & Dental: The university maintains clinics on campus for basic health services and medical clearance for enrollment.

    Scholarships: As a state university, NORSU falls under the Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act (Free Tuition Law) for eligible undergraduates. Additional scholarships (CHED, DOST) are processed through the Scholarship Office.

4. Admission Essentials

Admission to NORSU follows a "liberal admission, selective retention" policy.

    General Requirements:

        Original Grade 12 Report Card (Form 138/SF9).

        Certificate of Good Moral Character (from school and barangay).

        PSA Birth Certificate.

        2x2 ID photos.

    Grade Requirements:

        General Average: Minimum of 80% for most courses.

        Board Programs (Engineering, Nursing, Accountancy, etc.): Typically require a minimum of 83% - 85% and no grade below 80% in specific subjects (Math/Science/English).

    Examinations: Applicants usually need to take the NORSU Admission Test (NAT) and, for some programs, the English Qualifying Examination (EQE) or a department-specific interview.
